Course 02

Social Media Basics

Before any client work, interns should understand what each platform is for, where the tools are, and how to move inside the app without guessing.

Instagram

Use it for profiles, reels, stories, DMs, insights, and content publishing. Learn the profile page first, then the settings, then the business tools.

Facebook

Use it for pages, business portfolio, ads manager, inbox, and account roles. It matters because some client workflows still rely on Facebook tools even when the audience is on Instagram.

UI basics

Know where buttons, menus, search, inbox, and settings are before you handle client work.

Business tools

Understand professional accounts, page access, roles, and insights instead of only personal posting.

Safety

Learn what to touch and what not to touch so interns do not break client settings by mistake.

Lesson guide

Start here: profile layout, bio, highlights, and where content lives on each platform.

Then learn: posting tools, stories, reels, community management, and messaging.

Then learn business use: settings, account access, role assignment, and insight reading.

Then practice: switch between personal and business thinking so you know what matters for a client account.

Common mistakes: clicking settings without knowing why, mixing personal content with business work, and missing messages or approvals.

Example

Instagram task

Check profile, open insights, review last 7 days, then note what content worked

Facebook task

Open page settings, confirm access, and review inbox or roles

YouTube task

Find channel settings, upload flow, and where comments or studio tools live
