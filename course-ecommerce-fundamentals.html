Course 14

E-commerce Fundamentals

E-commerce is the full system of selling products online. Interns should understand the store, the product, the supplier, the delivery, and the customer after the sale.

What it is

A digital store where people browse, compare, and buy products without entering a physical shop.

Sourcing

Finding the right product, supplier, quality, price, and stock plan before selling.

Delivery

Moving the order from warehouse or supplier to the customer on time.

Lesson guide

A basic flow: list the product, accept the order, confirm stock, ship it, and support the customer if there is a problem.

Dropshipping: the seller does not keep the product in hand; the supplier ships it after the order comes in.

Fulfillment: the process of packing and delivering orders correctly and on time.

Why it matters: even if the store looks beautiful, bad sourcing or slow delivery can break trust quickly.

Interns should learn: product basics, pricing logic, shipping steps, returns, and customer expectations.

Example

Good flow

Product chosen, supplier checked, order received, packed, shipped, customer updated

Bad flow

Product listed without stock check, order delayed, customer left guessing

Dropshipping note

Useful for low stock risk, but support and supplier quality still matter
