Design Basics

Good design is not decoration. It is how you guide the eye, reduce confusion, and make the message easy to read.

Hierarchy

Show what matters first, second, and third.

Spacing

Give content room so it can breathe.

Typography

Choose fonts and weights that match the message.

Lesson guide

What design does: it helps people know where to look, what to read, and what to do next.

How to start: put the most important thing first, keep labels short, and avoid crowded sections.

What interns should learn: hierarchy, spacing, alignment, consistency, and color use before they touch fancy effects.

Adobe basics: know which tool is for what, instead of opening random apps and guessing.

Common mistakes: too many fonts, too many colors, and no clear focal point.

Example

Bad

Every line is loud, nothing is clear

Better

One headline, one supporting line, one action

Good

Consistent spacing, readable type, and calm layout
