CSAT Basics

CSAT is a simple way to know if the customer felt helped, respected, and satisfied after an interaction.

What it measures

It measures customer feeling, not just whether the ticket was closed.

Where it appears

It can appear after support, delivery, or any service touchpoint.

Why it matters

It shows if the customer experience was actually good, not just finished.

Lesson guide

Good CSAT comes from: quick replies, respectful tone, helpful answers, and clear next steps.

Bad CSAT comes from: being rude, slow, vague, or sounding like you do not care.

Support teams use CSAT to learn: what customers like, what confuses them, and where service breaks down.

Interns should learn: that closing a ticket is not the same as solving the customer’s problem well.

Fixes that improve CSAT: empathy, follow-up, correct routing, and simple language.

Example

Low CSAT reply

Your issue has been noted. Wait for update.

Better reply

I understand the issue. I am checking this now and will update you with the next step.

Best reply

I understand the issue and I am checking it now. I will update you by [time] with the next step.
